FAQ's

We want you to ask questions.
In today’s busy world, everyone is rushing from one event to another. However, time with your internal medicine doctor or general practitioner should never be hurried.Seeking --- and heeding --- health care advice is vital for your health.
Even having standard awareness & knowledge will go a long way. Here is a cluster of FAQs regarding ordinary illnesses and some bonus questions boosting your medical knowledge.

  • What can I do to improve my health?

    The common standbys are always true: Diet and exercise are key ingredients in a healthy lifestyle. A good night’s sleep is also vital for good health.

  • What general dietary habits should I follow?

    To start off, skip soda as much as possible. It contains 200-300 calories per can and is in no way beneficial for health. Only eat nuts that are good for health and they include walnuts, almonds and unsalted peanuts. Never skip breakfast and pack it with an army of whole grains & fruits.

  • What measures should I take to keep my mental health & stress in check?

    Include those activities in your schedule which help you relax. They may include deep breathing, yoga or meditation. They have an added benefit as a blood pressure regulator. Furthermore, visit a psychologist at regular intervals.

  • Does ingesting psychiatric drugs alter my personality or classify me as “crazy”?

    Psychiatric drugs don’t alter your personality altogether, however, they will alleviate symptoms which required you to go to the doctor in the first place. NO, availing psychiatric care does not classify you as “crazy”. Some individuals require more care than others but, in general, these patients are indistinguishable from the general public.

  • How to cure headache quickly & effectively?

    Headache can be cured by many methods. Some of which are listed below.
    • Using a cold pack or a hot compress
    • Massaging the scalp using essential oils
    • Dimming the lights & avoiding chewing
    • Increase water intake & have caffeine
    • Contact your local doctor if it persists

  • How to aid a person who got from a heatstroke?

    To start off, immerse the patient in cold water & then wrap them in cooling blankets & ice to lower their body temperature. Visit your doctor after providing the above first aid.

  • What are the risks coupled with obesity?

    Obesity is a rapidly growing illness in India and is even reaching epidemic proportions. 12.1% of males & 16% of females in India are obese. It places you at risk for several conditions including:
    • Heart disease
    • Type 2 diabetes
    • Cancer
    • High Blood Pressure
    • High cholesterol
    • Stroke

  • What are a few prescribed ways to lose weight organically?

    There are many methods of weight loss which include:
    • Try intermittent fasting
    • Track your diet and exercise
    • Have plenty protein in breakfast
    • Cut back on fats & refined carbs 
    • Get a good night’s sleep

  • How often should I get my teeth checked?

    The basic rule of thumb says you should visit the dentist every six months. Studies have shown that people without any problems are ok to go once a year while those with dental issues should get checked out every 3 or 4 months.

  • What first aid should I provide to a person suddenly cracking a bone or spraining a joint?

    Firstly, stop any visible bleeding using a sterile bandage or a clean cloth. Immobilise the injured area and apply ice wrapped in a towel onto the injured skin. Finally, wait for help!

  • How to treat a pulled muscle?

    Apply ice wrapped in a cloth to the swelled region. Rest the pulled muscle for a day or two and not use it while it is still painful.
